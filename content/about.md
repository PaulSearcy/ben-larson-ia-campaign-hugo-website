## Roots in Rural America

I grew up on an acreage along the Platte River in rural Nebraska. My mother's family were ranchers; my father's side served as teachers, law enforcement, and public employees. From an early age, I developed a deep appreciation for our land, our communities, and the working families who sustain them.

When I was 16, I lost my father to an aggressive form of cancer. The compassionate care he received from emergency medical technicians inspired me to pursue a career in healthcare. I graduated from Westside High School in Omaha in 2010 and immediately enrolled in the Paramedicine and Emergency Medical Sciences program at Metropolitan Community College, earning my degree in 2013.

## A Career Serving Iowans and Nebraskans

At just 20 years old, I was working 911 as a paramedic in Auburn and Falls City, Nebraska. I witnessed firsthand what happens when essential services are privatized: within a year of corporate acquisition, 911 services in these communities were shut down entirely. This experience taught me that some things are too important to be left to the profit motive alone.

I became an advocate for working people, organizing alongside my coworkers for safe equipment and fair treatment. I also stood with Nebraska farmers during the Dakota Access Pipeline protests when the state attempted to seize private land for corporate interests.

## Frontline Experience Through the Pandemic

While working toward my nursing degree, I served at Select Specialty ICU during the COVID-19 pandemic. Our team specialized in ventilator care and advanced respiratory therapies. We maintained rigorous safety protocols that protected both patients and staff, even as we faced threats for doing so. By the end of 2020, we had cared for families who lost multiple members to the virus.

In 2021, I moved to Council Bluffs—returning to the area where my parents grew up—and enrolled in the RN Program at Iowa Western Community College. I worked full-time while completing my degree, graduating in 2023 with an Associate's in Nursing. Today, I serve as a cardiac care nurse within the Nebraska Medical System.

## Why I'm Running

Over more than a decade in emergency medicine, ICU care, and specialty nursing, I've seen our healthcare system from every angle. I can speak with authority about what works, what's broken, and what we need to fix. I've watched hospitals close, ambulance services collapse, and insurance companies deny care to patients who desperately need it.

I'm not running to talk about problems in the abstract. I'm running because I've lived them—and because Iowans deserve a representative who understands the real challenges facing working families, healthcare workers, and rural communities.

## Beyond the Campaign

When I'm not working or campaigning, you'll find me pursuing my interests in astronomy, renewable energy technologies, gardening, and working on projects in my garage. I believe in building things—whether that's an electric bike, a community garden, or a better future for Iowa.
